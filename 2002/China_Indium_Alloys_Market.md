Technological advancements focus on developing alloys with enhanced thermal and electrical properties, with new formulations offering 25% improved thermal conductivity compared to 2020 standards. The market is seeing a shift towards lead-free indium alloys, growing at 10% annually due to global environmental regulations. The market faces challenges from price volatility of raw indium, but the growing adoption of indium alloys in advanced packaging technologies continues to drive innovation. Research into new indium-based shape memory alloys is emerging as a promising field, with potential applications in actuators and sensors growing at 20% <strong>CAGR</strong>, albeit from a small base. Looking ahead, the market is expected to benefit from the increasing use of indium alloys in next-generation solar cells, with indium-gallium-based multi-junction cells projected to grow at 15% <strong>CAGR</strong> through 2030.
