 <p><span style="font-size:14px">PVC homopolymer (Polyvinyl Chloride homopolymer) is a widely used plastic material primarily known for its rigid and durable characteristics. It is derived from the polymerization of vinyl chloride monomer (VCM) and is utilized in a variety of end-use applications due to its strength, chemical resistance, and ease of processing. PVC homopolymer is distinct from its copolymer counterpart, which includes other monomers mixed with vinyl chloride for added flexibility. However, PVC homopolymer maintains a solid, rigid form that is ideal for a broad range of structural and industrial applications.</span></p>
 
 
 
 <p><span style="font-size:14px">Typical uses of PVC homopolymer span numerous industries such as construction (pipes, siding, fencing), automotive (interior trim, dashboards), medical (medical devices), and electrical (wires and cables). Its versatile properties make it an essential material for many products that require durability, weather resistance, and structural integrity.</span></p>
