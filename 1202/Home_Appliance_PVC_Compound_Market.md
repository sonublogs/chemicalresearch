 <p>Home appliances such as refrigerators, washing machines, air conditioners, and televisions often require specialized materials that offer durability, flexibility, and resistance to wear and tear.  <p>PVC compounds can be categorized based on their plasticizing properties. <strong>Non-plasticized PVC compounds</strong> are rigid and offer structural integrity, while <strong>plasticized PVC compounds</strong> are more flexible and are often used in applications requiring greater flexibility and durability. The market for PVC compounds in home appliances is expected to grow steadily due to the increasing demand for durable, cost-effective, and energy-efficient products.</p>
